Contributions

In 1642 to 1644, Blaise Pascal crafted the Pascaline. Its main job was to help his father, its second job was to oversee tax computations in Rouen. The Pascaline itself was the first working mechanical calculator, relying on integer-based input. Pascal gave his invention to Pierre Seguier, the Chancellor of France, in 1644.

Legacy

Blaise Pascal left a legacy in mathematics, science, and philosophy. He invented the Pascaline, an early calculator, and contributed to probability theory. He created Pascal's law, which remains important in physics. His impacted literature through Provincial Letters. Overall, Pascal's genius has influenced many fields and continues to inspire today.
